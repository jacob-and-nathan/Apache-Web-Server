Tip #1: Underpaintings.

Underpaintings, are a (usually watery) wash of a small amount of colours that are done before the main painting.

They are good because they provide a good basework for the rest of the painting.

To do one, paint the WHOLE canvas with a colour (for example Burnt Umber).
Once you have completed it, you can start your main painting!

Acrylic is a good medium to do this with, as it dries extremely fast.

Tip #2: Experiment with different mediums.

Painting mediums are fluids, that can be mixed with the paint, to give a specific effect.

The effect could be drying faster or slower, or changing the consistency.

Tip #3: Invest in some good quality art supplies.

It can make a substancial difference if you buy some better quality art supplies.
If you don’t know what to invest in first, here is my advice:

START with whatever is the most important. For example paper, or a pallete.
THEN, get some better quality brushes.
THEN, get some better paints.
AFTER that, get anything else that you want.

You don’t HAVE to follow this, but that is a suggestion.
